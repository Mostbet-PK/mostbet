Short slot questions from PK readers

What does volatility actually mean for a slot session?

It describes how the slot spreads wins over time. Low volatility means smaller wins arrive more often, while high volatility means longer cold stretches between rarer larger hits. It does not predict the outcome of any single session.

Is a higher RTP always a better choice on a slot?

Higher RTP is favourable on paper, but the figure is measured over millions of spins. Two slots with similar RTP can feel very different in practice because of their volatility and bonus structure.

Why do slots count fully for bonus wagering when live tables often do not?

The contribution percent is set by the promo rules and reflects the house edge of each category. Slots usually carry a higher built-in edge for the operator, which is why their wagering contribution is the largest of all.

Are free spins worth more on high-volatility slots?

Not by default. High-volatility slots can produce larger wins but also long cold runs. Free spin value depends on the bet per spin, the cap on winnings and the wagering rules attached, not on volatility alone.
