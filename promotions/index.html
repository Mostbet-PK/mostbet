Short promotion questions from PK readers

Why do bonuses often look larger than they end up being?

The headline figure usually shows the percent or the spin count, while the real value depends on the rollover, the eligible titles and the cashout cap further down inside the rules page. Reading the small print before the headline gives a more honest expectation.

Is a higher percent always a better promotion?

No. A larger percent paired with heavy wagering and a short deadline can end up worth less than a modest percent with reasonable rules. The promo stages matter more than the banner figure on its own.

Can two promotions be active on the same account at the same time?

Usually not. Most platforms apply a one-active-bonus rule, with the next opt-in waiting until the previous wagering is either cleared or cancelled inside the account.

What happens to leftover bonus funds once the deadline runs out?

Unused bonus credit and unfinished wagering progress are typically forfeited when the deadline expires. Any real-money winnings already released earlier usually stay on the account balance.
